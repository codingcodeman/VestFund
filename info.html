Learn About Investments

Stocks

Stocks are shares of ownership in a company. Investing in stocks allows you to earn returns through capital gains and dividends.

Roth IRA

A Roth IRA is a retirement account where you contribute after-tax income, and withdrawals are tax-free after age 59Â½.

Traditional IRA

Contributions to a Traditional IRA may be tax-deductible, and investment grows tax-deferred until withdrawal.

Life Insurance

Provides a lump sum to your beneficiaries upon your death and can be used as a long-term investment tool with cash value growth.

Mutual Funds

Mutual funds pool money from many investors to buy a diversified portfolio of stocks, bonds, or other securities.

Index Funds

Index funds replicate the performance of a specific market index like the S&P 500, offering low-cost diversification.

Bonds

Bonds are fixed-income securities that represent a loan from an investor to a borrower, usually a corporation or government.

ETFs

Exchange-Traded Funds are similar to mutual funds but trade on stock exchanges. They offer diversification and low costs.
